Ingredients:
 - 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
 - 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
 - 1 tablespoon ground cumin seeds
 - 1 tablespoon ground coriander seeds
 - 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
 - 2-3 tablespoons shahi paneer masala (I like this one - http://www.amazon.co.uk/MDH-Shahi-Paneer-Masala-100g/dp/B00TIAJK5O/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1455311185&sr=8-1&keywords=mdh+shahi+paneer)
 - 2 white onions, diced
 - 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
 - 3-4 chillies, minced
 - 1-2 cm ginger, minced
 - 1 tin plum tomatoes, blitzed
 - 2 tablespoons tomato concentrate
 - 100ml double cream
 - 1kg paneer, diced
 - 3-4 tablespoons olive oil
 - 30g butter
 - 1 tablespoon salt

Directions:
 1. Heat the olive oil and butter over a medium heat. When hot, add the cumin
    and mustard seeds. Fry quickly for a minute or two.
 
 2. Add the diced onion, and cook until browned. Stir occasionally.
 
 3. Add the minced garlic, ginger, chilli and ground coriander seed, cumin
    seeds and turmeric. Fry for a few minutes until cooked through.

 4. Add the blitzed tomatoes and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat if required.
    Add the shahi paneer masala and a 100ml of water. Bring back to the simmer
    and let it simmer for a few minutes.

 5. Add the paneer, and let it cook through. Stir occassionally.

 6. Add a tablespoon of salt, stir through, then add cream. Bring back to a
    simmer for a few minutes, then serve.
